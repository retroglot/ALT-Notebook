> "Studies that involve human participants must be co-designed with community members."
> `“涉及人类参与者的研究必须与社区成员共同设计。”`

---

In September, the US government announced a one-off US$495-million funding boost for historically Black colleges and universities (HBCUs) and tribal colleges and universities (TCUs). This is welcome news – but doesn't redress their chronic underfunding.

> 在 9 月，美国政府宣布向历史上的黑人学院和大学（HBCUs）以及部落学院和大学（TCUs）一次性增加 4.95 亿美元的资金支持。这一消息令人欣慰——但并未解决这些院校长期面临的资金不足问题。

In the United States, HBCUs educate 1.5% of all university students – yet receive only 0.9% of federal research and development (R&D) funding. Data reported in 2024 show combined HBCU endowment assets around $100,000 per student. By contrast, the ten wealthiest US universities received $2 million to $7 million per student in 2023.

> 在美国，HBCU 培养了全国 1.5% 的大学生，却仅获得 0.9% 的联邦研发资金。2024 年报告的数据显示，HBCU 合并后的捐赠基金资产约为每名学生 100,000 美元。相比之下，2023 年美国最富有的十所大学每名学生获得的捐赠资金在 200 万至 700 万美元之间。

As a scientist and administrator at Meharry Medical College – a 150-year-old private historically Black health-sciences institution in Nashville, Tennessee, with some 1,500 students – I've seen how hard it can be to manage on scant budgets. I urge the government to redress funding levels. In the meantime, HBCUs and TCUs can target their investments, as Meharry has done with some success.

> 作为田纳西州纳什维尔市 [[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 的科学家兼行政人员——这所拥有约 1500 名学生的私立传统非裔健康科学院校已走过 150 年历程——我深切体会到在有限预算下维持运转的艰难。我敦促政府调整对传统非裔院校与原住民部落学院的资助水平。与此同时，这些院校可以效仿 [[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 已取得一定成效的做法，实施精准投资策略。

Over the past decade, Meharry has increased its funds for research, and its scholarly output, by using metrics to guide budgeting and investments. We tracked publications and citations, grants, awards, student engagement, graduation and career placement rates, facility use, data generation and sharing (how many samples were processed, what data sets were produced and how were shared), research collaborations and investigator diversity. We also expanded our number of sponsors and monitored how funds were used.

> 在过去的十年中，[[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 通过运用指标来指导预算编制与投资，从而增加了研究资金并提升了学术产出。我们追踪了出版物与引用量、资助项目、奖项获得情况、学生参与度、毕业与就业安置率、设施使用率、数据生成与共享情况（如处理了多少样本、生成了哪些数据集以及如何共享）、研究合作以及研究人员的多样性。同时，我们还拓展了赞助方数量，并持续监控资金的使用情况。

Between the periods of 2010-15 and 2020-25, the number of cross-disciplinary collaborations increased, both between groups at Meharry and with external investigators. The number of papers with Meharry authors indexed in the PubMed database of biomedical literature rose from 444 to 1,067.

> 在 2010-15 年至 2020-25 年期间，跨学科合作的数量有所增加，这既包括 [[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 内部团队之间的合作，也包括与外部研究者的合作。收录于生物医学文献数据库 PubMed 中、由 [[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 作者撰写的论文数量从 444 篇上升至 1,067 篇。

Three measures underpin this growth. First, we established transdisciplinary pilot grants of 50, 000− 75,000 per faculty member lasting for 12-18 months - funded by federal research endowments or private foundations - to seed ideas and support core staff and infrastructure. While maintaining budgets for ongoing cancer, HIV and oral-health studies, we prioritized new projects in genomics, inflammation, immunity and cardiometabolic health. We also launched two new schools: one in applied computational sciences, in 2021; and one in global health, in 2023.

> 三项措施支撑了这一增长。首先，我们设立了每位教员 50,000 至 75,000 美元的跨学科试点资助项目，为期 12-18 个月——资金来源于联邦研究基金或私人基金会——用于孵化创意并支持核心人员与基础设施。在维持现有癌症、艾滋病及口腔健康研究预算的同时，我们优先启动了基因组学、炎症、免疫及心脏代谢健康领域的新项目。我们还于 2021 年成立了应用计算科学学院，并于 2023 年创立了全球健康学院。

Second, we invested in infrastructure to handle, store and analyse data securely. We established procedures – including some around human consent and data sovereignty – that meet both federal and Tribal Nations' standards. Studies that involve human participants must be co-designed with community members to ensure that any concerns – for example, about privacy, potential stigma or data misuse – are addressed early on. Steps such as pre-consented sharing of electronic health records or clinical notes can help to accelerate the analysis of disease risk, treatment response and gaps in care, to inform services and policies that benefit local people. This transparent and respectful approach deepens public confidence in science, especially among populations long marginalized in research.

> 其次，我们投资建设了安全处理、存储和分析数据的基础设施。我们建立了一套符合联邦和[[Tribal Nations|部落国家]]标准的程序——包括涉及人类同意和数据主权的相关规定。任何涉及人类参与者的研究都必须与社区成员共同设计，以确保尽早解决各类关切——例如隐私、潜在污名化或数据滥用等问题。采取诸如预先同意的电子健康记录或临床笔记共享等措施，有助于加速分析疾病风险、治疗反应和护理缺口，从而为惠及当地居民的服务和政策提供依据。这种透明且尊重的方式增强了公众对科学的信任，尤其是在长期被边缘化的研究群体中。

The same principles guide Meharry's participation in country-wide programmes funded by the US National Institutes of Health that are aimed at training and upskilling researchers in population genomics and clinical science, and embedding artificial-intelligence tools in biomedical research and health care. Priorities include addressing under-representation in data and algorithmic bias affecting, for example, Black, Hispanic/Latine, Pacific Islander and Native American communities. Meharry focuses on building trusted community partnerships and improving data governance, access and analytics through secure cloud-based workspaces. One project brought community members and data scientists together to develop AI-enabled music-based therapy for pain, stress, memory loss and low mood.

> 同样的原则指导着 [[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 参与由美国国立卫生研究院资助的全国性项目，这些项目旨在培训和提升研究人员在群体基因组学与临床科学方面的能力，并将人工智能工具融入生物医学研究与健康护理之中。优先事项包括解决数据代表不足以及算法偏见问题，这些问题尤其影响黑人、西班牙裔/拉丁裔、太平洋岛民和美洲原住民等社区。[[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 着重于建立可信的社区伙伴关系，并通过安全的云端工作空间改进数据治理、访问与分析能力。其中一个项目汇聚了社区成员与数据科学家，共同开发基于人工智能的音乐疗法，以应对疼痛、压力、记忆力减退和情绪低落等问题。

Our third measure was to adopt cross-disciplinary mentoring. We pair each graduate student and early-stage faculty member with three mentors: one in their domain (for instance, cancer), another in a methodological discipline (such as AI and bioinformatics) and a third with expertise in translational or clinical research or community engagement (a physician, for example). This approach ensures scientific rigour, enriches projects by providing diverse perspectives and grounds studies so they have real-world applicability.

> 我们的第三项措施是采用跨学科指导机制。我们为每位研究生和早期职业教师配备三位导师：一位来自其专业领域（例如癌症研究），另一位来自方法论学科（如 AI 与生物信息学），第三位则具备转化或临床研究及社区参与的专业经验（例如理疗师）。这种方法既保证了科学严谨性，又通过多元视角丰富了项目内涵，同时使研究扎根实际，具备现实应用价值。

Exchange programmes with industry and peer universities, including partners elsewhere in the United States and in Africa, also serve to expand skills and cross-disciplinary networks. For example, Meharry's Together for CHANGE public-private partnership with a biopharmaceutical consortium of Regeneron, AstraZeneca, Novo Nordisk and Roche supports genomic discovery for populations of African ancestry. This project is building a genome database of 500,000 participants – the largest international undertaking so far – to redress the long-standing marginalization of global Black populations in pharmaco-medical research.

> 与行业及同类高校的交流项目，包括美国其他地区及非洲的合作机构，同样有助于拓展技能与跨学科网络。例如，[[Meharry Medical College|梅哈里医学院]] 与 Regeneron、AstraZeneca、Novo Nordisk 及 Roche 组成的生物制药联盟共同发起的 "携手共创变革" 公私合作项目，致力于推动非洲裔人群的基因组发现。该项目正在构建一个包含 500,000 名参与者的基因组数据库——这是迄今为止规模最大的国际性科研行动，旨在纠正全球黑人群落在药物医学研究中长期被边缘化的状况。

Building accountability is crucial, too. Metrics are shared through dashboards, quarterly open briefings, annual public seminars, news reports and website postings. And scientists interact with communities through project-specific advisory boards, town-hall meetings and feedback surveys.

> 建立问责机制同样至关重要。我们通过仪表盘、季度公开简报、年度公开研讨会、新闻报道及网站发布等渠道共享各项指标。科学家们还通过项目专属咨询委员会、市政厅会议及反馈调查等方式与社区保持互动。

Governments, funders and policymakers must support resilient networks of community-rooted institutions. Investing in them is not charity: it is a strategic imperative for global discovery, prosperity and human well-being.

> 各国政府、资助机构及政策制定者必须支持扎根社区的韧性机构网络。对这些机构的投入并非慈善行为，而是关乎全球探索发现、经济繁荣与人类福祉的战略要务。
